Yogurt is a fermented milk product made by combining specific starter bacteria, primarily Streptococcus thermophilus and Lactobacillus delbrueckii subsp. bulgaricus. It is known for its smooth texture and mildly sour flavor due to lactic acid content. There are two common styles of yogurt: set-style, which is poured into containers to set, and Swiss-style, which is stirred before packaging.
